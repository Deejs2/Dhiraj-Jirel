Switzerland Of Nepal

July 25, 2024 by Dhiraj Jirel

Jiri (Nepali: जिरी) is a municipality in Dolakha District in the Bagmati Province of central Nepal. At the time of the 2011 Nepal census, it had a population of 13,638 people. Jiri, which lies about 190 kilometers from Kathmandu, is the main gateway to the Everest Region.

There are two government high schools namely Jiri Higher Secondary School at Hatanda and Dhungeshwori Secondary School at Jiri Bazaar. One private boarding school called JEM Academy, and two community schools, Martyrs Memorial School and Saraswoti Community School. Jiri Technical School provides technical education in agriculture, construction, and health.

Who Are Jirels?

The Jirels (Nepali: जिरेल जाति) are an ethnic Kirati group, one of the 59 indigenous peoples in Nepal. They like to be called Jirpa, which means "leopard-like people." Jirpa believe they migrated to Jiri and Dolakha from the western part of Nepal. The name Jiri derives from Jirpa.

The Jirels are the original inhabitants of Jiri and its adjoining villages in the Dolkha district, central Nepal. They have their own distinct language, culture, customs, rituals, and lifestyles. They are mostly Buddhist but also adhere to other religions such as Christianity, Islam, and Hinduism. They also worship Jhakri (shamans). Their indigenous shamanistic beliefs are centered on practitioners called Phombo, who are believed to have a direct relationship with the supernatural world.

Their main occupation is agriculture and animal husbandry. In recent years, they have also been involved in business, government, and teaching jobs. Many Jirels serve in the British Gurkha Army, Indian Army, Nepalese Army, and the police. They are primarily settled in Dolakha, Sindhupalchowk, Chitwan, and Ilam districts, near the Likhu, Khimti, Jiri, and Jiri Shikri rivers. Jirels have linguistic and ethnic semblance with the Sherpas and the tribes of Central Tibet. Their mother tongue, Jirpa Kecha, belongs to the Tibetan language family.

Popular Places in Jiri

Cherdung Danda

One of the most breathtaking spots in Jiri is Cherdung Danda. At an altitude of 3200 meters above sea level, it offers a mesmerizing view of the Himalayas. The journey to Cherdung Danda starts from Jiri's Linkan Bazar and takes approximately six hours of hiking. The effort is well worth it, as the panoramic views from the top are simply awe-inspiring.

Kalo Bhir

On the way to Cherdung, you'll encounter Kalo Bhir, another magnificent natural landmark. Kalo Bhir is a massive rock that stands majestically on a cliff. The hike to Kalo Bhir from Jiri's Linkan Bazar takes about four hours. From the top, you can see the major towns of Charikot, Dolakha, and Jiri all at once. The steep landscape and strong winds make standing on top quite challenging, but the experience is exhilarating.

Stone Park

Stone Park Jiri is a beautiful park located 25 kilometers from Linkan Bazar. It features large rocks, sitting areas, picnic spots, and a beautiful outdoor pool. It's a perfect place to spend unforgettable time with family and friends.

Jiri Fish Pond

Jiri's heart, Linkan Bazaar, is one of the famous towns in the Dolakha district. It is bustling with shops and a variety of attractions, including the Jiri Fish Pond. In addition to its commercial vitality, Linkan Bazar is home to significant landmarks such as the Jiri Technical School, Boudha Stupa, and a statue of Rimporche. The Jiri Hospital is also located here, serving the local community and visitors alike.
